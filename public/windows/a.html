Specification

Profile

Energy efficient window, made of extruded, impact-resistant and climate-resistant PVC profile with very good insulating properties, which provide good u-value. The U-value for the window is determined by the insulating ability of the glass. Enova recommends windows with 3-layer energy-saving glass. Frame depth 90 mm. PVC profiles are completely recyclable.

  • + Great, classic design
  • + Good U-value
  • + Maintenance-friendly
  • + Long service life
  • + Recyclable

Hot-dip galvanized steel reinforcements

The reinforcements are installed into profiles for extra strength and stability.

Fittings

High quality fittings from Spilka in steel (St.37). The fitting is electrolytically galvanized (zinc layer min 15 Âµm), gold chromated and finally dipped in clear lacquer. The visible part in the closed position is powder coated in white. All holes in the joint connections are internally polished and lubricated. The fittings have been tested and approved in accordance with requirements from NDVK (Norwegian Door and Window Control). Corrosion tests show that the fitting exceeds the requirements for such components by a good margin.

Automatic safety and air barrier

The combined air ventilation and safety locking function launches automatically when the window is opened. The H-window + top swing has security on all fittings from a height of 388 mm to 1588 mm.

Air ventilation

All our windows can be supplied with a Spilvent Click-vent in the upper frame. The Click-vent is very tight in the closed position, and in the open position it leads the air into the room.

Lock

All our windows are supplied with children security and can be also supplied with a lockable handle with a key.

Installation

Screws, anchors or adjufix.

Combinations

The window can be delivered in various combinations with fixed and opening windows in a frame.

Decorative bars

Our windows can be supplied with decorative bars that can be glued on both sides of the glass, Georgians bars, removable Vindex bars or a combination.
